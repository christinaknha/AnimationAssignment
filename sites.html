Things to do in Portugal

There are plenty of things to do in Portugal but depending on how much time you have, the following three activities are do-able within a day each.

Exploring Downtown Lisboa

We set up homebase in Lisboa. It's where our airport was and is a big enough city that there is reliable transportation to get to and from other parts of Portugal without it being a hassel. It was also big enough that there was plenty to do if we did not feel like traveling too far outside.

There were a plethora of things to do in downtown Lisboa from seeing history sites and parks to shopping on Principe Real. Each street had plenty of hole-in-the-wall restaurants/cafes to try. If you ever got lost, public transportation was always nearby or you could call an Uber for less than $2.00!

Hiking in Cascais

Cascais is a beach town a short train ride away from Lisboa. You can explore the quaint city streets and allyways, eat at any of the restaurants with a view of the coast, or hang out on the beach. But if you ask me, the one thing you need to make sure you do is take the scenic trail to Boca do Inferno, also known as the mouth of hell. There are plenty of places to stop for a photo op and the views are phenomenal.

Exploring the Palaces of Sintra

Although I said Sintra could be a day trip, you won't be able to see everything in one day. It will require you to take the train to the mountanous regions of Portugal. From there, you will have to either catch the bus, a Tuktuk (highly suggest), or an Uber to get to where the palaces are. From there, there are so many palaces to see and explore. You will do a lot of hiking. I suggest making sure you are wearing comfortable clothes, bringing sunscreen, and carrying some water.
